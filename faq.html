To get the most accurate estimate, you will need the specific medical or procedure codes used by PhilHealth.

1. Identify your Case Type
PhilHealth categorizes cases into two types:
Medical Cases (ICD-10): These are for illnesses (e.g., Pneumonia, Dengue, Hypertension). They use ICD-10 codes, which are typically alphanumeric (e.g., A90 for Dengue Fever). Surgical/Procedure Cases (RVS): These are for operations or medical procedures (e.g., Appendectomy, Dialysis, Cesarean Section). They use RVS codes, which are 5-digit numeric codes (e.g., 59510 for a C-Section).
2. Where to Find These Codes?
If you are currently at the hospital or have already been treated, you can find these codes in the following places: Ask Your Doctor: Ask your attending physician for the Final Diagnosis (ICD-10) or the Procedure Code (RVS). PhilHealth Claim Form 2 (CF2): If you have a copy of your discharge papers or PhilHealth forms, look for the "Case Rate Code" section. Billing Statement: Some hospitals include the ICD-10 or RVS code next to the PhilHealth deduction on your statement.
3. Searching in the Estimator
Step 1: Enter the ICD-10 or RVS code directly into the input field for the fastest result.
Step 2: If you don't have a code, try searching by Description (e.g., "Pneumonia"). Note that descriptions must match PhilHealth’s official terminology to appear.
Step 3 (Optional): If you have two conditions, enter the second code in the "Second Case" field. The tool will automatically calculate 100% of the more expensive case and 50% of the cheaper one.
4. Check Your Facility Level
Ensure you know if your hospital is Level 1, 2, or 3. Rates for the same illness often differ based on the hospital's equipment and specialization level.
